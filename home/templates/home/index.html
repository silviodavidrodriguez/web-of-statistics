Free Online Statistical Tools for ANOVA, Regression, Probability, Inference, Control Charts, and Multivariate Analysis.

Web of Statistics is a free online platform offering statistical calculators for ANOVA, regression analysis, probability distributions, descriptive statistics, control charts, and multivariate analysis. It is designed for students, researchers, and professionals who need fast and reliable tools for data analysis.

You can perform statistical calculations online without installing software, including hypothesis testing, data visualization, and advanced statistical methods.

Learn more about the platform in the documentation, watch tutorials on our YouTube channel @WebofStatistics, or explore example datasets in this Google Sheet.

This website was created by Dr. Silvio D. Rodríguez, Professor of Applied Statistics at the Pontificia Universidad Católica Argentina (UCA), Buenos Aires, to provide free and accessible statistical tools for educational purposes. Its goal is to help students, researchers, and professionals solve academic problems and strengthen their understanding of statistical concepts.

To keep this ad-free platform running, donations are welcome and help cover hosting and maintenance costs, ensuring these resources remain freely available to all.

Feedback, bug reports, and suggestions are always welcome and help improve the platform. Please contact webofstatistics@gmail.com with comments or ideas.

Note: This website is currently optimized for desktop and laptop use.

About the author

Dr. Silvio D. Rodríguez is Professor of Applied Statistics at the Pontificia Universidad Católica Argentina (UCA), Buenos Aires, and Associate Researcher at CONICET. He holds a Ph.D. from the University of Buenos Aires in Industrial Chemistry and has extensive academic and research experience in statistical analysis, chemometrics, multivariate methods, and data interpretation applied to science and engineering.

Web of Statistics was developed as an academic initiative to make statistical tools more accessible to students, teachers, researchers, and professionals.
